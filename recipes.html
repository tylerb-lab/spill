Classic Flat White

Double ristretto, 150ml silky steamed milk. The one Australians think they invented.

Time4 min
DifficultyEasy
Yield1 cup

You'll need

18g espresso beans (medium roast)

150ml whole milk (or oat milk)

Espresso machine + steam wand

01

Pull a double ristretto (18g in, ~25g out, 20-22 seconds). Preheated cup.

02

Steam 150ml milk to 60-65°C. You want silky microfoam — no big bubbles.

03

Pour milk from low height, cut through the espresso. A flat white should be velvety, not frothy.

Cold Brew Concentrate

12-hour steep, zero heat, maximum vibes. Make a batch Sunday, thank yourself all week.

Time12 hrs
DifficultyEasy
Yield600ml

You'll need

100g coarsely ground coffee

600ml cold filtered water

Large jar + fine mesh strainer

01

Combine coarse grounds (1:6 ratio) with cold water in a large jar. Stir briefly.

02

Cover and refrigerate for 12-16 hours. Don't over-steep or it gets bitter.

03

Strain through fine mesh (and paper filter if you want clarity). Store up to 2 weeks.

04

Serve 1:1 with water or milk over ice. This is concentrate — don't drink it straight unless you want to vibrate.

Aeropress Banger

The nerd's secret weapon. Cheap, portable, and will embarrass your espresso machine.

Time5 min
DifficultyMedium
Yield1 cup

You'll need

15g medium-fine ground coffee

200ml water at 90°C

Aeropress + paper filter

01

Rinse paper filter. Set Aeropress in inverted position. Add 15g grounds.

02

Pour 200ml water at 90°C. Stir gently. Place cap with rinsed filter.

03

Wait 2 minutes. Flip onto cup. Press slowly over 30 seconds. Stop when you hear hiss.

04

Dilute to taste with hot water. This is espresso-strength concentrate.

Pour Over V60

Slow, meditative, and produces a clarity of flavour that makes you emotional.

Time8 min
DifficultyMedium
Yield300ml

You'll need

18g medium-fine ground coffee

300g water at 92°C

V60 + paper filter + gooseneck kettle + scale

01

Rinse filter with hot water. Discard rinse water.

02

Add grounds. Pour 40g water for bloom. Wait 30 seconds.

03

Pour slowly in circles to 150g, then continue to 300g. Keep consistent pace.

04

Total drain time: 3-3.5 minutes. Too slow = grind coarser. Too fast = grind finer.

Espresso Tonic

Two shots over tonic water and ice. Sounds wrong, tastes like a revelation.

Time3 min
DifficultyEasy
Yield1 drink

You'll need

Double espresso (36-40ml)

120ml quality tonic water (Fever-Tree or similar)

Ice, tall glass

01

Fill tall glass with ice. Pour in cold tonic water.

02

Pull double espresso. Let it cool for 30 seconds.

03

Pour espresso slowly over the back of a spoon onto the tonic. Let it float briefly, then stir.

Iced Matcha Latte

Ceremonial grade, oat milk, and enough ice to make you forget it's Monday.

Time3 min
DifficultyEasy
Yield1 cup

You'll need

2g ceremonial grade matcha

60ml hot water (75°C, not boiling)

200ml oat milk, ice, bamboo whisk

01

Sift matcha into bowl. Add hot water. Whisk in W/M motions until frothy with no lumps.

02

Fill glass with ice. Pour cold oat milk over ice.

03

Pour matcha concentrate over the top. Stir before drinking.
